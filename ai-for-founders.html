AI for founders · 6-week workshop

Build an AI agent that knows your business.

Most people use AI like a chatbot. That can be useful, but it only goes so far.

This workshop helps founders build something more useful: an agent that understands the company, works with real context, and helps with real work. When AI has the right context, memory, tools, and workflows, it stops being a toy and starts becoming useful.

At a glance

Who this is for

Built for founders who want AI to become part of how the company actually works.

This workshop is designed for founders and operators who want to move from generic prompting to practical systems: one real use case, one working agent, and one clear path to broader adoption.

Too much stays generic

Most AI use never gets past prompting, because the system does not know the company well enough to be useful.

Context lives in people

Product context, customer insight, and decision logic stay trapped in the founder instead of being structured for reuse.

Tools exist, systems do not

Teams try many AI tools, but without memory, access, and workflow design, they do not turn into useful operating systems.

What participants leave with

One practical agent, one working workflow, and a roadmap for what to do next.

  • One clear AI use case for the business.
  • One agent configured around real company context.
  • One working workflow connected to real work.
  • A roadmap for where AI can create the most leverage next.
Program

Six weeks. One real agent. Built around your company.

The program is built around a simple idea: a model by itself is not that useful. It becomes useful when you connect it to your business.

Week 1

From chatbot to agent

Mental model
Participants work on
  • What AI can and cannot do well today.
  • The difference between a model, a tool, and an agent.
  • Why most founder use stays shallow.
  • Choosing one real use case to build.
Outcome
  • Clear use case selection.
  • Shared framework for context, memory, tools, and workflows.
  • Stronger sense of where AI can create leverage.
Week 2

Teach the agent your business

Context
Participants work on
  • Defining the company, product, customer, and priorities.
  • Capturing tone, decision patterns, and operating context.
  • Assembling the first knowledge base from real materials.
  • Deciding what the agent should know by default.
Outcome
  • First business context layer.
  • Useful source material structured for reuse.
  • Agent instructions grounded in the actual company.
Week 3

Connect data and systems

Tools
Participants work on
  • Deciding which documents, tools, and data sources matter.
  • Connecting the systems that support the chosen use case.
  • Structuring information so the agent can retrieve and use it well.
  • Avoiding overload and noisy inputs.
Outcome
  • Useful access to the right systems.
  • Cleaner inputs and retrieval logic.
  • Working base for the first real workflow.
Week 4

Build one real founder workflow

Workflow
Example

Sales and outreach

  • Research and outreach preparation.
  • Proposal and follow-up generation.
  • Repeatable founder sales support.
Example

Meetings and decisions

  • Meeting synthesis and action tracking.
  • Decision memory.
  • Delegation support.
Example

Founder communication

  • Writing support.
  • Customer and stakeholder updates.
  • Voice-aware drafting.
Week 5

Make the system improve over time

Memory
Participants work on
  • Deciding what the agent should remember.
  • Saving useful context and removing noise.
  • Improving prompts, instructions, and outputs.
  • Adding review loops where human judgment matters.
Outcome
  • More reliable output quality.
  • Cleaner memory and feedback loops.
  • Clearer path from demo to repeated use.
Week 6

Capstone and roadmap

Adoption
Participants work on
  • Presenting the agent and workflow.
  • Reviewing what worked and what did not.
  • Identifying the next highest-leverage use cases.
  • Creating a practical adoption roadmap for the company.
Outcome
  • One working company-aware agent.
  • One workflow tied to real work.
  • Clear next steps for broader AI adoption.
Format & approach

Hands-on. Practical. Built around real company context.

This is not a lecture series and not generic prompt training. Each session combines short framing, live examples, guided implementation, and outputs participants can use inside the company right away.

Format

6 weeks. 1 live session per week. 2 hours per session. Light async work between sessions.

Participation model

Solo founders can join, but founder-operator pairs often get the strongest implementation outcomes.

Suggested materials

Bring company docs, notes, transcripts, sales materials, examples of good output, and any systems relevant to the use case.

Final promise

This workshop helps founders move from generic prompting to one practical system that understands the business and supports real work.

The goal is not to make participants experts in tools. The goal is to help them leave with one agent, one workflow, and a clear sense of where AI can create the most leverage next.
